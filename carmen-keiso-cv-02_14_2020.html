BIO
Carmen-Sibha Keiso is a multimedia artist and curator working in performance, video, and text. Keiso’s practice functions
as a reflexive mise-en-scène, wherein current socio-political and global cultural industries are fused with the semantics of
social performativity. Chiming millennial diaspora with contemporary cultural trends as an attempt to divulge the way we
analyse the self within these spheres. Keiso follows a collaborative and research based process, wherein they direct a
form of 'theatre' involving practitioners from varying disciplines.
Keiso co-progenerates ‘Read the Room’, an expanded literary practice space that hosts writing workshops, talks,
readings, and exhibitions in Melbourne. In addition to the release of a feature length film in 2018, Keiso has sustained a
documentative film practice, collaborating with local artists in creating documentary work, such as ‘MeowTV’ and ‘The
WetBody Tour’. Keiso recently presented a lecture as part of the Writing & Concepts 2019 series on July 20 at SIGNAL
artspace and is currently undertaking a short-course at HB Studios, as well as a mentorship with artist Keren Cytter, as
part of a professional development opportunity funded by the Ian Potter Cultural Trust.
